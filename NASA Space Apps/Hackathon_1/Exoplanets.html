Exoplanets: A Glimpse into Other Worlds

Exoplanets, are planets existing outside the Solar System. Most orbit stars, but those which don't i.e., they float freelly are called Rogue Planets.

  • First possible yet unrecognised existence of an exoplanet was in 1917,
  • First detection however was in 1988, and confirmed in 2003.

    • Exoplanets, or extrasolar planets, are planets located outside our solar system, orbiting other stars. The study of exoplanets has rapidly evolved over the past few decades, providing insights into their formation, characteristics, and potential habitability. They're believed to form from the protoplanetary disks of gas and dust that surround young stars. Through processes like accretion and collision, these materials gradually coalesce into planetesimals, eventually forming planets.

    Currently, we have about 4 ways to find an exoplanet:

  • Transit Method: Observing the dimming of a star’s light as a planet passes in front of it.
  • Radial Velocity Method: Measuring the wobble of a star caused by the gravitational pull of an orbiting planet.
  • Direct Imaging: Capturing images of exoplanets by blocking out the star’s light.
  • Gravitational Microlensing: Observing light from distant stars that gets bent by the gravitational field of an exoplanet.
